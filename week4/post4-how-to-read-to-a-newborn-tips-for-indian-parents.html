For example, a study published in the Journal of Applied Developmental Psychology found that Indian children who were read to regularly from birth had better reading comprehension skills in school.

Introduction to Reading

Reading to newborns is an essential activity that exposes them to different sounds, rhythms, and speech patterns, which helps in developing their phonological awareness. The IAP recommends reading to babies from birth, as it has a significant impact on their cognitive, social, and emotional development. In the first few months, babies are learning to recognize sounds and develop their sense of language, making reading a critical activity for their development.

Benefits of High-Contrast Images

High-contrast images are ideal for newborns, as they can see clearly only within 20–30 cm. These images help in developing their visual skills and hand-eye coordination. A study by the NICHD found that high-contrast images activate 2x more neural connections in the visual cortex than color-based alternatives. In India, where the majority of the population has limited access to quality educational resources, high-contrast images can be a valuable tool for developing newborns' visual skills.

Tips for Reading to Newborns

Reading to newborns can be a fun and engaging experience for both you and your baby. Here are some tips to keep in mind: - Read with enthusiasm and expression to keep your baby engaged - Choose books with simple, repetitive text and high-contrast images - Read regularly, even if it's just for a few minutes a day - Make reading a special bonding time with your baby
