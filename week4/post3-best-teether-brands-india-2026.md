## What Makes a Safe Infant Teether in India?

Before comparing brands, understand the baseline for safety in the Indian market:

**BIS IS 9873 Certification** — This is the Bureau of Indian Standards mandatory safety certification for rubber and plastic teethers sold in India. It covers material safety (BPA, phthalates, lead), mechanical durability, and choking risk. Under Indian consumer protection law, teethers sold in India should comply with IS 9873.

**Food-Grade Silicone** — The safest teether material for infants. Non-toxic, temperature-stable, and free from the chemical leaching risks associated with PVC or low-grade rubber.

**One-Piece Construction** — No detachable parts that can become choking hazards. The entire teether should be a single moulded unit.

**Age Appropriateness** — A safe **newborn teether** for 3–6 months has different requirements than a teether for 8–12 months. Choose based on your baby's current age and grip development.
