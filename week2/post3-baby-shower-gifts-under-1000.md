## What to Look for in a Baby Shower Gift (Practical Checklist)

Before we get to the list, here is what separates a genuinely useful gift from one that collects dust:

- **Age-appropriate:** A gift should be usable from birth, or close to it.
- **Developmentally relevant:** It should support a specific skill — sensory development, motor skills, visual stimulation.
- **Safe materials:** For any product touching a baby's skin or mouth, look for BIS certification (Bureau of Indian Standards) or food-grade material certification. In India, the IS 9873 standard covers toy safety for young children.
- **Practical and reusable:** Something the family will reach for daily, not once.
- **Easy to clean:** New parents do not have time for hand-wash-only items.

---

## What to Avoid Gifting at a Baby Shower

A quick word on what to skip, so you do not inadvertently create stress for a new parent:

- **Strong-fragrance products** — Newborn skin is sensitive. Heavily fragranced oils, soaps, or room sprays can trigger reactions.
- **Toys with small parts** — Any detachable piece is a choking hazard for babies under 3 years. Check the age label.
- **Non-certified toys** — Cheap plastic toys with no certification may contain phthalates or BPA. If the toy has no safety label, skip it.
- **Amber teething necklaces** — These are popular in some circles but are a documented choking and strangulation hazard. Paediatric associations including the IAP advise against them.
- **Another set of newborn clothes** — Unless you know exactly what is needed, there is a very high chance of duplication.

## A Note on Safety Certifications

When you are gifting any product that a baby will mouth, touch, or sleep with, the material certification matters. In India, the Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) certifies products against the IS 9873 standard for toys and baby products. For teethers and sensory toys specifically, food-grade silicone with BPA-free and phthalate-free certifications is the benchmark to look for.
