Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best way to clean a cloth book?

Cloth books can be easily cleaned with a soft cloth and mild soap. Make sure to rinse the book thoroughly and allow it to air dry to prevent any damage to the materials. The NICHD recommends that parents wash their baby's toys and books regularly to prevent the spread of germs and infections.

Can I use a cloth book during tummy time if my baby has sensitive skin?

Yes, cloth books can be a great option for babies with sensitive skin. Look for books made from soft, gentle materials that are hypoallergenic and fragrance-free. The IAP recommends that parents choose products that are gentle on their baby's skin and can help reduce the risk of skin irritation.

How often should I read to my baby?

The IAP recommends that parents read to their babies regularly, starting from birth. Aim to read to your baby at least once a day, and make it a fun and engaging experience by using different voices and sounds. The WHO recommends that parents read to their babies for at least 10-15 minutes a day to help develop their language skills and promote a love of reading.

Can I use a cloth book during tummy time if my baby is less than 3 months old?

Yes, cloth books can be used during tummy time for babies less than 3 months old. However, make sure to start with short sessions and gradually increase the time as your baby gets older and more comfortable with tummy time. The NICHD recommends that parents start tummy time with short sessions of 2-3 minutes and gradually increase the time as their baby gets older.

Are cloth books safe for my baby to mouth?

Yes, cloth books are safe for your baby to mouth. Look for books made from non-toxic materials and made with safe inks. The BIS recommends that parents choose products that meet the safety standards for toys and books, and that are made from non-toxic materials.
