Why Format Matters for Newborn Visual Development

According to the National Institute of Child Health and Human Development (NICHD), newborns are born with a limited range of vision, and high contrast images and patterns help stimulate their visual development. The IAP recommends using high contrast visual aids, like cloth books, to promote healthy vision development in newborns. With specific statistics showing that high contrast images activate 2x more neural connections in the visual cortex, it's clear that the right format matters.
