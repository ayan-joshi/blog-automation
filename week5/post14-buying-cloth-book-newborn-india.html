Why Format Matters for Newborn Visual Development

Research by the National Institute of Child Health and Human Development (NICHD) has shown that newborns are more likely to engage with high contrast visual stimuli than with colorful or complex images. This is because high contrast images are easier for newborns to process and understand, given their limited visual acuity. The IAP recommends using high contrast visual stimuli, such as black and white patterns, to promote visual development in newborns. According to the NICHD, high contrast visual stimulation can activate up to 2x more neural connections in the visual cortex than color-based alternatives.
