Why Format Matters for Newborn Visual Development

According to the National Institute of Child Health and Human Development (NICHD), newborns are born with a limited range of vision, and their visual development is influenced by the type of visual stimulation they receive. High contrast images, such as those found in cloth books, are more effective at capturing a newborn's attention and promoting visual development. The IAP recommends using high contrast visual stimulation tools, such as cloth books, from birth to 6 months.
